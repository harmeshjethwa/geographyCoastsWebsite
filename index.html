Coastal management

Groynes

Groynes are used to stop long shore drift moving sand and material along the coast.

Advantages
Creates a wider beach
Provide useful structure for people interested in fishing
Less expensive than other methods
Disadvantages
Starves beaches further along the coast leading to increased rates of erosion elsewhere

Rock armour

Absorbs wave energy by breaking the wave.

Advantages
Cheap and easy to maintain
Can provide interest to the coast
Often used for fishing
Disadvantages
Rock is usually from other parts of the coastline or even abroad which can be expensive to transport
Often does not fit with local geology
Can be very obtrusive

Gabions

Provide a buffer against the sea or to support a cliff.

Advantages
Cheap to produce and flexible in final design
Can improve drainage of cliffs
Can eventually become vegetated and merge into the landscape
Disadvantages
For a while they look very unattractive
Cages only last 5-10 years before they rust

Beach nourishment

Adding sand or shingle to make a beach higher or wider.

Advantages
Blends in with existing beach
Increases tourist potential
Disadvantages
Needs constant maintenance unless structures are built to retain the beach
Expensive
